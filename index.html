What I Do

Air Pollution Detection and Prediction

• Created a PyTorch-based LSTM model to forecast air pollution levels (PM2.5, NO2, CO) with metrics, utilizing meteorological factors and time-series data for improved accuracy. Applied data preprocessing techniques, including feature scaling, time series decomposition, and handling missing values, to optimize LSTM model performance and enhance prediction reliability.
• Also published this paper on Oct 7 2023, in International Journal on Recent and Innovation Trends in Computing and Communication.

Geospatial Analytics for Supplier Performance Evaluation.

Designed a geo-ML pipeline combining OpenStreetMap, OSRM, Haversine with advanced ML techniques (PCA, K-Means, Hierarchical, DBSCAN, Association Rule Mining, Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes, Decision Trees, SVM, AdaBoost, Random Forest, Bagging) to uncover hidden supplier risk patterns and revealed Air shipments (~58.7%) as the dominant mode shaping global supply chains.

LLMPerformance Comparison and Analysis

• Analyzed performance metrics for large language models (LLMs) across various benchmarks, using Random Forest, XGBoost, and CatBoost for multi-class classification on a dataset with 50,000+ entries.
• Conducted hyperparameter tuning and visualized with confusion matrices, enhanced model selection by identifying efficient architectures for specific tasks.

Plant Disease Identification and QA System.

• This project aims to solve that gap by not only classifying plant diseases from images, but also generating natural language answers to user questions using a domain-specific fine-tuned language model.
• Timely identification and treatment of plant diseases is critical to preventing crop loss and improving agricultural productivity. Most existing systems stop at disease classification — leaving users without actionable insights.

Predicting US Workforce Automation Risks

• Developed a predictive model to assess workforce vulnerabilities to automation in the U.S., identifying high-risk occupations using a dataset with 700+ occupations, automation probabilities and state-level employment data.
• Employed Bernoulli Naive Bayes classification, Chi-Square, ANOVA, and other machine learning techniques to predict automation risk, achieving 93% accuracy.
